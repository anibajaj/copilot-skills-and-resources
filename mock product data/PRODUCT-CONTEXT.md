# HeroForce — Product Context

> A short briefing so any agent (or new teammate) can understand what HeroForce is,
> who it's for, and how people actually use it day to day.

---

## 1. What HeroForce is

**HeroForce is a B2B SaaS CRM** (customer relationship management platform) for sales
teams. Companies buy it by the **seat** (one seat = one licensed rep) and use it to
manage their sales **pipeline** — the set of open deals moving from first contact to
close. It's a cloud product: reps sign in through a browser or desktop app, and every
deal, contact, activity, and forecast lives in one shared workspace.

The product's promise: **help reps find the right deal to work on, log what they did,
and close faster** — increasingly with the help of an AI copilot (**AI Assist**).

**Category:** Sales CRM / revenue platform
**Model:** Multi-tenant SaaS, per-seat subscription, three plan tiers
**Primary job-to-be-done:** *"Show me what to work on next and help me close it."*

---

## 2. Who it's for

### Buyers & plans
Customers are **companies**, and they land on one of three plans:

| Plan | Who it's for |
|------|--------------|
| **Starter** | Small teams adopting their first CRM, a handful of reps |
| **Growth** | Scaling sales orgs with multiple teams and integrations |
| **Enterprise** | Large orgs needing advanced security and high seat counts |

Sold globally, weighted toward the Americas, with a growing EMEA and APAC presence.

### End users (the people in the app)
- **Sales reps / Account Executives** — the core daily user. Work their pipeline, open
  deals, and log calls, emails, and meetings.
- **RevOps / Sales Operations** — configure pipelines, dashboards, and integrations;
  keep an eye on team health and forecast accuracy.
- **Sales managers / VPs** — review team pipeline, coach reps, and read forecasts.

---

## 3. Core features

1. **Pipeline** — the visual board of open deals by stage. The rep's home base.
2. **Deal records** — one record per opportunity: value, stage, contacts, activity
   history, and close date.
3. **Activity logging** — reps log calls, emails, meetings, and notes against a deal.
4. **Global search** — an omnipresent search bar to jump to any account, deal, or
   contact.
5. **Data-sync integrations** — two-way sync with email, calendar, and external systems.
6. **Reporting & forecasting** — dashboards for managers and RevOps.
7. **AI Assist** — an AI copilot embedded in the deal record and pipeline. It suggests
   next best actions, drafts follow-ups, and recommends which deals to prioritize; reps
   accept suggestions inline. It's the newest and fastest-growing surface.
8. **Reliability & support** — a highly available platform with responsive support.

## 4. Login & onboarding experience

**Sign-in.** Reps reach HeroForce via a company sign-in (SSO for Growth/Enterprise;
email + password for Starter). After auth they land on the **rep home** — a personalized
"what to work on today" view: a pipeline summary, deals needing attention, and an
**AI Assist** panel with suggested next actions.

**Onboarding.** New customers go through seat provisioning: an admin invites reps, and
each rep sets up their profile and connects email + calendar so activity can sync.

**First-run flow for a rep:**
1. Accept invite → set up profile / connect email + calendar.
2. Land on rep home → see their assigned pipeline.
3. Open a deal (from the pipeline board, a "needs attention" item, a notification, or
   search).
4. Log the first activity.
5. Try an AI Assist suggested next action.

---

## 5. The daily-use flow

This is the heartbeat of the product — what an active rep does **every day**:

1. **Log in** and land on the rep home.
2. **Find the work.** Reps reach a deal any of several equally valid ways: clicking a
   deal off the pipeline board, following a "needs attention" item, opening a
   notification or emailed link, using a saved view, or searching.
3. **Do the work.** Update a deal, log a call/email/meeting, or advance a stage — often
   with AI Assist drafting the update or suggesting the next action.
4. **Come back tomorrow** to keep the pipeline moving.

The same shape shows up in the first-run flow above: reps arrive, reach their deals,
take an action, and return.
